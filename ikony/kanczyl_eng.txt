lat. Tragulus javanicus</br>
de. Tragulus javanicus</br>

The smallest hoofed mammal in the world, weighing only 2–2.5 kg.
It inhabits tropical forests with dense undergrowth.
Its diet consists of fallen fruits, buds, young twigs, and fungi.
Males have elongated canines and mark their territory with glands located under the chin.
They live alone or in pairs. After a five-month pregnancy, the female gives birth to a single young, which is a miniature version of the adult and reaches maturity at about six months.
Their lifespan can reach up to 12 years.</br>
