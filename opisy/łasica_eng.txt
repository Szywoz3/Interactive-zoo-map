lat. Mustela sibirica</br>
de. Sibirischer Wiesel</br>
ua. Мустела сибірська</br>

Distribution: Eastern Asia — from the Sea of Okhotsk to southern China, the Gobi Desert, Korea, and Taiwan</br>

Habitat: Mountainous, forested slopes and tundra areas; sometimes rice fields</br>

Diet: Small mammals such as voles, mice, squirrels, amphibians, and fish</br>

There are 11 recognized subspecies of the Siberian weasel.

In winter, it develops very thick and fluffy fur. The guard hair (outer coat) can reach up to 4 cm in length, and the underfur is also very dense.

It moves swiftly on the ground, can climb trees when needed, and is also an excellent swimmer.</br>
