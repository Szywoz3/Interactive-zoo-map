lat. Diceros bicornis</br>
eng. Black rhino</br>
ukr. Black rhinoceros</br>

WESTERN SUBSPECIES - completely extinct.</br>

EASTERN SUBSPECIES - the population numbers only about 8 thousand</br>

Black rhinoceroses feed on LEAVES, TWIGS AND GRASS TIPS. Their mouth is adapted for browsing: the triangular upper lip overlaps the lower one.</br>

PREGNANCY in the black rhinoceros lasts about 15 months, always one calf is born, weighing between 20 and 25 kilograms. The "little one" stays with the mother for about 2 - 3 years.

The Zoological Garden in Poznań is the ONLY one in Poland where two species of rhinoceroses live.

Do you want to know how to get from here to the white rhino enclosure - take a look at the map.</br>
