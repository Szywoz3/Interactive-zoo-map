lat. Helogale parvula</br>
de. Südliche Zwergmanguste</br>
ua. Карликові мангусти</br>

Distribution: Eastern and Central Africa (south of the Sahara)</br>

Habitat: Savannas and semi-deserts</br>
Diet: Insects, small mammals, and smaller reptiles</br>

It is the smallest carnivore in Africa.

The dominant role is held by the female.

In the group, only the offspring of the alpha pair (usually the oldest male and female) survive.

Thanks to their thick fur and acetylcholine receptors, they are resistant to snake venom. Snakes are the main targets of their hunts.

Active during the day. They live in family groups of up to 30 individuals, with a strict hierarchy within the group.</br>
