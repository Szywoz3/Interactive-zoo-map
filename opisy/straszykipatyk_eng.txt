Insects - Insecta</br>

South-East Asia</br>

These exotic herbivorous insects are a classic example of mimicry, meaning they resemble their natural surroundings in appearance (color and shape).
Some species look like green leaves (leaf insects), dry leaves (Australian stick insect), pieces of bark (Malayan stick insect), or twigs (stick insects).
Another defensive reaction of stick insects is the ability to enter a state of immobility called catalepsy or akinesis.</br>
