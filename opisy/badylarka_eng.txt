lat. Micromys minutus</br>
de. Eurasische Zwergmaus</br>
ua. Мишка лучна</br>

Distribution: Eurasia — from northern Spain and Great Britain to Mongolia, China, Korea, and Japan</br>

Habitat: moist meadows with tall grasses or shrubs, farmland, forests, and railway embankments</br>

Diet: grass and cereal seeds, insects, berries, buds, and plant shoots</br>

It uses its prehensile tail to grasp grass stems while collecting food.

It builds small, spherical nests (up to 7 cm in diameter) on grass stems, 20–100 cm above the ground.

It does not hibernate in winter.

During cold periods, it moves to warmer places with access to dry grasses and grains.</br>
