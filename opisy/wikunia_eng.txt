Lat. Vicugna vicugna</br>
De. Vikunja</br>
Ua. Вікунья</br>

Distribution: Peru, Bolivia, Chile, Argentina</br>

Habitat: dry and cold climates, at high altitudes (3,500–5,750 m a.s.l.)</br>

Diet: grass, leaves, and shrub buds</br>

The smallest member of the camelid family, closely related to the guanaco and llamas.

They are sensitive to thunderstorms; when sensing approaching lightning, they lie flat on the ground.

The vicuna is depicted on the coat of arms of Peru.

Some indigenous tribes honor the vicuna, considering it a daughter of the fertility goddess.</br>
