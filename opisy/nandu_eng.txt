Lat. Rhea americana</br>
De. Nandu</br>
Ua. Нанду великий</br>

Distribution: Argentina, Bolivia, Brazil, Paraguay, and Uruguay</br>

Habitat: meadows, savannas, grassy wetlands</br>

Diet: plants, seeds and fruits, reptiles, insects, scorpions, and small rodents</br>

A single nest can contain 10 to 30 eggs (from different females). Incubation is done by the male.

It uses its wings for sudden changes of running direction and to confuse predators.

Compared to the African ostrich, it has three toes on its feet and is significantly shorter, reaching up to 140 cm in height.</br>

RED LIST:</br>
Near Threatened – NT</br>
