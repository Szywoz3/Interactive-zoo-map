lat. Spalacopus cyanus</br>
de. Coruro</br>
ua. Коруро</br>

Distribution: Central Chile, South America</br>

Habitat: From mountainous areas through dry savannas to dunes and sandy forests</br>

Diet: Mainly geophyte tubers; less often herbs, grass shoots, bark, and cacti</br>

Rarely comes to the surface and feeds exclusively underground.

Stores collected bulbs and tubers in its burrows for the winter.

Lives in colonies of up to about 15 individuals, composed of several pairs and their young.</br>

Least Concern – LC</br>
