lat. Oryx gazella</br>
eng. Gemsbok</br>
de. Spießbock</br>
ua. Гемсбок</br>

distribution: southwestern Africa</br>

habitat: dry grasslands, deserts</br>

diet: grasses, herbs, buds, fruits, roots</br>

Both males and females have horns, which can reach up to 120 cm in length.

A strict hierarchy exists within the herd, overseen by one dominant and very territorial male.

They can dig for water with their front hooves, sensing it beneath the sand in dry riverbeds.

Gemsboks can go without water for several days and are also true masters at locating it.</br>
