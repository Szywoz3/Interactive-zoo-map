lat. Dromaius novaehollandiae</br>
de. Großer Emu</br>
ua. Ему австралійський</br>

Distribution: Australia</br>
Habitat: open plains</br>
Diet: grass, seeds, fruits, insects</br>

It is unique that the male alone takes care of the chicks.

The emu cannot fly but runs very well and can reach speeds of up to 50 km/h.

It is the second-largest bird in the world after the ostrich. The male lays from 4 to 12 eggs.

During the year, the emu can travel up to 1,000 km. The incubation period lasts 56 days. The male does not eat during incubation.</br>
