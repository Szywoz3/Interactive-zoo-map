NORTHERN SUBSPECIES – the last male, Sudan, died in March 2018. The last two females of this subspecies live in the Ol Pejeta Reserve in Kenya.

SOUTHERN SUBSPECIES – inhabits savanna regions in the southern part of Africa.

White rhinos are grazers – their wide mouths are perfectly adapted to feeding on grass.

They are also the largest of all rhino species. Adult males can weigh up to 2,500 kg.

Pregnancy in the white rhino lasts 17–18 months. A single calf is born, usually weighing between 45 and 55 kg. The young stays with its mother for about 2–2.5 years.

The Poznań Zoo is the only zoo in Poland that houses two species of rhinos.</br>
