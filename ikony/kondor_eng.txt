lat. Vultur gryphus</br>
de. Andenkondor</br>
ua. Кондор андійський</br>

Distribution: Andes mountains, from Venezuela to Cape Horn</br>

Habitat: mountainous areas above the tree line, treeless rocky terrain, grasslands</br>

Diet: carrion, eggs of other birds</br>

They are able to soar for long periods without flapping their wings, using thermal updrafts.

They are among the longest-living birds in the world. Some individuals may live up to 70 years.

Condors living in larger colonies form social structures. This is evident in the hierarchy of access to food: larger and older males have priority.

They choose a mate for life and breed once every two years. The young learn to fly at around six months of age but remain under parental care until the next chicks appear in the nest.</br>
