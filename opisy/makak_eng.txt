The Japanese macaque, also known as the snow monkey, is a monkey species native to Japan.
It is characterized by a pinkish-red face, thick grey-brown fur, and a short tail.</br>
They live in troops led by dominant males. These monkeys are very intelligent – they often wash their food in rivers before eating it.</br>
In winter, they can often be seen sitting in hot springs.</br>
They inhabit both subtropical and subalpine forests, feeding mainly on fruits, seeds, insects, tree bark and roots. They can weigh up to 18 kg and live up to 30 years.</br>
Females are smaller than males.</br>
