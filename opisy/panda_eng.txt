lat. Ailurus fulgens</br>
de. Kleine Panda</br>
ua. Мала панда</br>

Distribution: Bhutan, China, India, Nepal, Myanmar (Burma)</br>

Habitat: Forests in mountainous regions</br>

Diet: Bamboo shoots, leaves, berries, small invertebrates, insects</br>

The red panda is more closely related to raccoons, skunks, and weasels than to bears. Scientists discovered this species about 50 years earlier than the giant panda.

The name "panda" comes from the Nepali words nigalya ponya, meaning "bamboo eater." In the wild, bamboo makes up about 95% of the red panda’s diet.

It spends most of its time in tree canopies, descending headfirst. Semi-retractable claws aid in climbing, while the underside of its paws is covered with dense fur for better grip and insulation.

The red panda population is rapidly declining due to habitat destruction and poaching.</br>

RED LIST:</br>
Critically Endangered – CR</br>
