lat. Phalcoboenus australis</br>
de. Falkland-Karakara</br>
ua. Ивичеста каракара</br>

Distribution: Falkland Islands and Tierra del Fuego (South America)</br>

Habitat: open lowland and rocky areas</br>

Diet: chicks, dead birds, insects</br>

It attacks newborn lambs and pecks at injured sheep, which led to persecution of this species by farmers.

Due to long island isolation, these birds do not show much fear of humans.

The name "Caracara" comes from the language of the Fupi tribe, meaning “to scream loudly.”

They have excellent color vision and sometimes only take red-colored objects.</br>

RED LIST:</br>
Near Threatened – NT</br>
