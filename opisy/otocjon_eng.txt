lat. Otocyon megalotis</br>
eng. Bat-eared Fox</br>
de. Löffelhund</br>

Distribution: Somalia, Sudan, South Africa, Angola, Zambia, and Tanzania</br>

Habitat: Dry areas and savanna</br>

Diet: Termites, large insects, fruits, eggs, and bird chicks</br>

Has 48 teeth – the most of any mammal.

A member of the dog family; due to its body structure and large ears (up to 12 cm), it is called the “bat fox.”

The name Otocyon comes from Greek: otus meaning ears and cyon meaning dog.

The ears allow it to detect sounds as subtle as the movements of insects underground.</br>
