lat. Casuarius casuarius</br>
de. Helmkasuar</br>
ua. Казуар шоломний</br>

Distribution: northern Australia, New Guinea</br>
Habitat: tropical forests and savannas near water</br>
Diet: fruits, occasionally small vertebrates and invertebrates</br>

The largest land animal of New Guinea.

The female lays up to 6 light green eggs, each weighing about half a kilogram. The male incubates them.

The cassowary is flightless. It uses its strong legs for defense, foraging, and traveling long distances.

The first individual of this species was brought to Europe in 1597 for Emperor Rudolf II of Habsburg.</br>
