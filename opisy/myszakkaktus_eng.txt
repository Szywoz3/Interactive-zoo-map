lat. Peromyscus eremicus</br>
de. Kaktusmaus</br>
ua. Кактусова миша</br>

Distribution: Southwestern United States, northern Mexico</br>

Habitat: Desert and semi-desert areas, rich in cacti, rocks, or shrubs</br>

Diet: Mainly fruits, flowers, and seeds; rarely insects and leaves</br>

Most active at dusk. Activity decreases during a full moon.

It is very fast – when escaping, it can run at a speed of 13 km/h, which is impressive given its size.

When frightened, it emits a high-pitched, piercing squeak.</br>

RED LIST:</br>
Least Concern – LC</br>
