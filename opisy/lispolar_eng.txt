Distribution: Arctic regions of Eurasia, North America, Greenland, and Iceland</br>

Habitat: Arctic tundra, coastal areas, and pack ice</br>

Diet: rodents (mainly lemmings), birds, eggs, carrion, fish, berries, and insects</br>

The Arctic fox occurs in two color morphs: white and blue.
It digs extensive burrows, sometimes used by many generations.
It usually forms monogamous pairs, with both parents caring for the young.
Thanks to its thick fur, fur-covered paws, and compact body, it is perfectly adapted to extreme cold.</br>
