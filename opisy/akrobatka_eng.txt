lat. Acrobates pygmaeus</br>
de. Schmalfuß-Federschwanz-Gleitbeutler</br>
ua. Акробатець карликовий</br>

Distribution: southeastern Australia</br>

Habitat: various types of forests, mainly eucalyptus and sclerophyll</br>

Diet: nectar, fruit juice, tree sap, insects</br>

The tail of the feathertail glider resembles a feather — it climbs trees using it like a prehensile grip.
It is used for steering during gliding flight, just like the gliding membrane stretched between the limbs.

The structure of the finger pads allows it to climb and move along thin trunks and branches, even on glass.

Despite its resemblance to rodents, the feathertail glider is a marsupial — the female carries her young in a pouch while they are still blind.</br>
