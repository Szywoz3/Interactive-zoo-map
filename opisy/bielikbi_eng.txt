lat. Haliaeetus albicilla</br>
de. Seeadler</br>
ua. Орлан-білохвіст</br>

Distribution: Europe and Asia</br>

Habitat: diverse landscapes (near lakes, rivers, wetlands)</br>

Diet: fish, water birds, small mammals, carrion</br>

The wingspan of the white-tailed eagle reaches 220–240 cm. Females have a greater wingspan than males.

It is one of the largest birds of prey in Northern Europe.

The white-tailed eagle is the symbol of Wolin National Park. In Poland, it is strictly protected by law.

They build their nests high in old trees (most often in the crown). White-tailed eagles are monogamous and pair for life.</br>
