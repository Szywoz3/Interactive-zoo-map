lat. Myrmecophaga tridactyla</br>
de. Großer Ameisenbär</br>
ua. Мурахоїд великий </br>

distribution: Central and South America from Honduras to Argentina</br>

habitat: jungles, mixed forests, grasslands, steppes, swamps</br>

diet: ants and insect larvae</br>

From each anthill it eats about 140 ants.
In a day it consumes around 30,000 insects. It avoids aggressive ants that produce irritant substances and form small colonies.

It has no teeth, and its long (up to 60 cm) sticky tongue allows it to extract ants and termites from their nests.

The young anteater (a miniature of the adult) climbs onto the mother’s back immediately after birth, where it stays for the first 6 months.

Powerful claws are used to dig into termite mounds and anthills, as well as for defense. While feeding, the long tongue extends 2–3 times per second.</br>
