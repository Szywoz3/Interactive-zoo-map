It feeds on flies and flying insects.
It resembles a dry twig (which provides advantages during hunting).
The female is 16 cm long, the male 13 cm. The wings cover the abdomen.
The head is triangular with eyes protruding to the sides. A fertilized female attaches the egg case to a twig.
The numerous newly hatched young lead an independent predatory lifestyle.</br>
