lat. Acomys dimidiatus</br>
de. Sinai-Sachelmaus</br>
ua. Голчаста миша східна</br>

Distribution: Middle East and southwestern Asia; areas of Sinai, Israel, Jordan, Saudi Arabia, Iran, and Pakistan</br>

Habitat: Dry forests, shrubby and rocky areas; sometimes cultivated fields and human houses</br>

Diet: Mainly seeds and insects; occasionally grass leaves</br>

Nocturnal; avoids high daytime temperatures.

Lives in small groups and is very social. Other female(s) help the mother in raising the offspring.

Its spiny fur helps avoid predation. In case of attack, it can also shed its tail.</br>
