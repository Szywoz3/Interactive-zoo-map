lat. Nasua narica</br>
de. Weißrüssel-Nasenbär</br>
ua. Носуха звичайна</br>

range: Central America </br>
habitat: tropical and mixed forests </br>
diet: fruits, seeds, invertebrates, bird eggs, amphibians, reptiles.</br>

They can easily climb down trees headfirst thanks to elbows that can rotate up to 180°. They are diurnal animals. Females with young form groups.

The tail helps to maintain balance while climbing.

The long snout makes it easier to obtain food in hard-to-reach places. In search of food, they are able to travel up to 2 km.</br>
