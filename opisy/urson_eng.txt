lat. Erethizon dorsatum</br>
de. Urson</br>
ua. Голкошерст канадський</br>

distribution: North America</br>

habitat: coniferous forests</br>

diet: parts of conifer trees, berries, roots</br>

It spends most of its life in trees, but it is also an excellent swimmer.

The upper part of the porcupine’s body is covered with quills equipped with barbs that break off easily.

During the day, it rests in caves or burrows. In winter, it does not hibernate.

Young are born with white quills, which darken over time. Adult porcupines have close to 30,000 quills covering their body.</br>
