lat. Sus cebifrons negrinus</br>
de. Visayas-Pustelschwein</br>
ua. Свиня вісайська</br>

Distribution: Negros Island, Philippines</br>

Habitat: rainforests</br>

Diet: omnivorous, mainly plant-based</br>

Distinct stripes and spots on the back are characteristic of young pigs.

The wild Visayan warty pig population numbers around 200 individuals.

The main threats to this species are deforestation, poaching, and hybridization with domestic pigs.

Boars (males) weigh up to 30 kg, while sows (females) up to 20 kg, making them among the smaller pig species.
A visible “crest” from the head to the back occurs only in males during the breeding season.</br>

RED LIST:</br>
Critically Endangered - CR</br>
