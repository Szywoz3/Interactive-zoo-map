lat. Budorcas taxicolor</br>
de. Takine</br>
ua. Такін</br>

Distribution: Himalayas</br>

Habitat: Forests and rocky valleys</br>

Diet: Leaves of shrubs and trees, grasses, and bamboo shoots</br>

The takin is the national symbol of Bhutan. In our Zoo, you can see 2 of the 4 subspecies:

-Golden Takin (Taxicolor taxicolor)

-Bhutan Takin (Taxicolor whitei)

Active in the early morning and late afternoon. Despite its build, it is very agile and capable of jumping.

In summer, it lives in herds of about 300 individuals. In winter, the group decreases to around 20, migrating from the mountains to lower areas with more abundant food.

In case of danger, it warns other herd members by making a cough-like sound.</br>
