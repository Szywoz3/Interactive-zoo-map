lat. Prionailurus bengalensis euptilurus</br>
de. Amurkatze</br>
ua. Амурський Леопардовий кіт</br>

Distribution: eastern Siberia, Manchuria, and Korea</br>
Habitat: forested areas</br>
Diet: rodents, birds, amphibians, fish</br>

The Amur leopard cat is one of ten subspecies of the Bengal cat.

Unlike most felines, males help care for their offspring.

Thanks to excellent climbing skills, they successfully hunt birds and bats.

They are primarily nocturnal; during the day they can only be observed when the temperature does not exceed +35°C.</br>
