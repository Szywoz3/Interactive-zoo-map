lat. Atherurus africanus</br>
de. Afrykanischer Quastenstachler</br>
ua. Їжатка африканська</br>

One of the largest rodents inhabiting Africa.
It spends its days in shelters, can climb trees, and is an excellent swimmer. It lives in family groups.
Its diet consists mainly of leaves, branches, roots, fruits, and plant tubers.
As in porcupines, some of its hairs are transformed into quills used for defense.
After a 3.5-month pregnancy, the female gives birth to one, rarely two young. It can live up to 23 years.</br>
