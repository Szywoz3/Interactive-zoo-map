lat. Arctictis binturong</br>
de. Marderbär</br>
ua. Бінтуронг</br>

Distribution: Indonesia (islands of Java, Sumatra, and Borneo), Indochina, Malaysia</br>
Habitat: Dense forests and tree canopies</br>
Diet: Omnivorous</br>

Binturongs lead an arboreal lifestyle. Their curved and large claws help them climb trees.

Binturong saliva has disinfecting properties – when applied to a wound, it significantly accelerates the healing process.

According to research conducted by Duke University, the scent of binturong urine resembles the smell of popcorn, due to specific chemical compounds found in it.

Their long and flexible tail helps them move among trees and hang from branches.</br>

RED LIST:</br>
Vulnerable – VU</br>
