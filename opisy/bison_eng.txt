lat. Bison Bison Athabascae</br>
de. Waldbison</br>
ua. Голкошерст канадський</br>

It belongs to the bovids.</br>

Due to mass hunting, its population declined sharply. It now lives primarily in national parks and reserves.

Two subspecies: wood bison – inhabiting sparse mountain forests, plains bison – inhabiting grassy prairies.

Adult weight: 800–1000 kg. Feeds mainly on grass. In winter, males and females with young form separate herds.

Usually, a single calf is born.

Wood bison – a species covered by the Washington Convention on international trade in endangered wild animals.</br>
