lat. Boselaphus tragocamelus</br>
eng. Nilgai</br>
de. Nilgauantilope</br>
ua. Нільгау</br>

Distribution: India</br>

Habitat: Open forests, sometimes open areas</br>

Diet: Grasses and herbs, twigs</br>

Hindus rarely hunt them due to their resemblance to sacred Indian cows (zebu).

These antelopes are mainly diurnal and live around 15 years. Their main predators are leopards and tigers.

Nilgai live in herds of 4 to 20 individuals. Older males sometimes adopt a solitary lifestyle.</br>
