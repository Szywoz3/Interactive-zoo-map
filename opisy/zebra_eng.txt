Lat. Equus grevyi</br>
De. Grevyzebra</br>
Ua. Зебра Греві</br>

Distribution: Kenya, Ethiopia</br>

Habitat: savannas, bushlands, agricultural and semi-desert areas</br>

Diet: leaves of shrubs and grasses</br>

Also called Grevy’s zebras. They are the largest zebras and the largest wild equids. They are also the most densely striped of all zebras.
Newborn foals have brown-black stripes, and their manes run along the back all the way to the tail.

They form large herds, often mixed with giraffes, antelopes, ostriches, and wildebeests. They nibble on dry, hardened grass tips that are too tough to be digested by other herbivores.</br>

RED LIST:</br>
Endangered – EN</br>
