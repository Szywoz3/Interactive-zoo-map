lat. Antilope cervicapra</br>
de. Hirschziegenantilope</br>
ua. Акробатець карликовий</br>

Distribution: Pakistan, Nepal, and India</br>

Habitat: grassy plains and wooded areas with access to water</br>
Diet: grasses, herbs, leaves, and tree buds</br>

The Indian antelope is also called the blackbuck.
The size of the herd depends on the availability of food and can range from a few to even 50 individuals.
Indian antelopes can run at speeds of 70–80 km/h.
Males have spirally twisted horns about 75 cm long.</br>
